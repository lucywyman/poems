On Unrequitted Love

I watch the way his mouth moves to form words
So that the first time we kiss his lips will already be as familiar as the sound of his voice
Like driving to high school again--natural, almost instinctual
As if we've done this a hundred times before.

I watch the way his hands grip a pencil
But there's so much to hate too
Each time he asks "Why doesn't she like me?", dismayed by rejection
Is a sucker punch to the gut
Making it hard to breath
His own questions echoing mine
Each of us wishing we were a little smarter, a little funnier, looked a little better naked
Feeding the illusion that these would make any kind of difference
Allow us to avert our gaze from the uncomfortable truth
"You aren't good enough"

I hate that there's a line I can't cross
That I have to swallow the words "I love you" each time we part ways
That I fall asleep alone
Maintain an air of casualty and nonchalance as he tells me he loves her
This other woman, with the most important person in my life wrapped around her finger
Not gonna lie, it's a little hard to handle.
