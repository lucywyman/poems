On God

Hail Mary, full of grace
Keep me safe in your embrace
When did life become this race
of who could wear the shortest skirts
and tightest shirts
and still be a virgin, chaste
Like you?

Amazing Grace
How sweet the sound
that condemned a wretch like me
Whose holy words
Left mine unheard
as though this were a travesty
That bore the cross
on my breast embossed
a red A for adultery

Our father, who art in heaven
I know I’m already seventeen
but Lord I just can’t understand why my mother’s so ashamed of me
Why the only thing that she can see
is not the love, or joy I felt, but the disgrace I brought on our family.
Why my friends look at me like an amputee
As if my virginity was a limb, now severed and lost
Why there’s an asterisk when they talk to me
the footnote reading “you slut”
Why no one stood by me, but instead erected “Do not enter” signs,
closed their pearly gates
and made our once mutual friendships into one-way streets
Why there is no water holy enough to wash me clean of these sins
No martyr who can save me,
Why my father looks at me so judgmentally
his deflowered little girl, Lucy
Where did we go wrong?
He asks.
And I don’t have to ask to know that I’m the mistake he’s talking about
Lord knows my parents never made love so consciously.

Dear Father, hear this:
When I had sex for the first time, I was not deflowered
I was reborn.
